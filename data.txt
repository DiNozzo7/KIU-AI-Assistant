SA : Student Assistant. A third or fourth-year student who conducts "seminars" (practical sessions). This position is paid.

TA : Teaching Assistant. A non-student assistant of a lecturer. Similar to an SA role. Usually has higher pay and is likely more professional. A TA typically manages more groups than a Student Assistant.

STA : Student Teaching Assistant, although usually referred to as a Student Tutor. A student who has already completed a course and helps other students successfully complete it. One of the ways to earn an ECAS point.

CE : Central Exercise. The practical part of a lecture where exercises are discussed. Usually conducted by the lecturer, or by a TA if the lecturer does not conduct it.

TTF : Test-Tutor Feedback. A period where students can ask questions to SAs/TAs. It is a practical session conducted in smaller groups (up to 25 students) instead of the entire course. Usually lasts one hour. There may also be a 20-30 minute test depending on the course.

Lab : Laboratory. Similar to TTF, but for programming courses. The standard duration was 2 hours.

ECAS Point : Required to become an SA starting from the third year. Without an ECAS point, a student cannot become a Student Assistant.

ECAS Point Acquisition : ECAS points can be earned by becoming an STA (Student Tutor) for a course or participating in Pi Day (only available for first-year students).

Multiple ECAS Points : Having multiple ECAS points does not provide significant benefits. It may only improve a LinkedIn profile by showing tutoring experience.
